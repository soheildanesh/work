TopicForest Side-by-side Comparison Against Google News, Perplexity and ChatGPT Deep Research in the Contexts of News, Academic Articles and General Web search
TopicForest is a research agent. You can give it a query and domain of query (E.g. news, web, scholar) and have it compile a mini-research report covering different sub-domain of the large domain of query. Below, you'll find side-by-side comparisons showcasing TopicForest's ability to provide more comprehensive, concise, and contextually rich information compared to existing solutions. These examples demonstrate our superior aggregation, synthesis, and organization capabilities across news, academic, and general web contexts. Note, these are static copies of TopicForest search result pages. To get to the actual page where TopicFOrest's "show more" links please click on the corresponding section header. Also note that Show More simply points at snippet of "thought" used by LLM to generate the specific summary paragraph.

Perplexity. Query 'Keen Triple beam Northbeam' - July 2 2025

TopicForest Web. Query: 'Keen Triple beam Northbeam' - June 30 2025

Insight: Niche Web Queries. TopicForest demonstrates its power in understanding and summarizing information on highly specific or niche topics, an area where generic search engines often struggle to provide cohesive results. Here I have used TopicForest as a first stop on learning about a new topic that has come up in my causal inference work: Multi-touch Attribution (MTA) in advertising. Asking a friend I was simply told TripleBeam and North Whale are the biggest players in the field. He was also interested in Keen so I included it. As you can see TopicForest's results do a great job of providing an overview of the general field of MTA before delving into subtopics with suggested searches for deeper dives.
Note, as TopicForest is in very early prototype stage in some cases we run in to errors retreiveing individual search results which gets reflected in the summary as seen in the above case. This is an exmple of simple engineering hurdles we will overcome quickly. Another feature that would be first up as the product matures is the 'show more' links currently submit LLM summary snippets as queries to Google. In the future they will link to the specific article the snippet was drawn out of along with other similar grounding human-generated documents.
